Vocaloids

More than voicebanks — these characters have grown into cultural icons with their own fanbases, aesthetics, and histories. Each one started as software. The community turned them into something much more.

Hatsune Miku

CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2007

The face of Vocaloid worldwide. She started as a voicebank and became a global phenomenon — holographic concerts, brand collaborations, and millions of fan-made songs. If you know one Vocaloid, it's her.

Kagamine Rin & Len

CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2007

Released as a pair and described as "mirror images" of each other. Rin is bright and energetic; Len is softer with a boyish tone. Beloved both individually and together — and the source of endless fan debate about their exact relationship.

Megurine Luka

CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2009

Deep, mature, and cool. Luka was the first Crypton Vocaloid to support both Japanese and English phonemes — a big deal at the time. Her elegant persona and versatile voice made her a favourite across genres from pop to electronica.

KAITO

CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2006

One of the earliest Vocaloids — predating Miku by a year. Initial sales were slow, but Miku's rise brought the whole CV Series back into the spotlight and KAITO found his audience. His warm baritone is unlike most Vocaloids and that's exactly why fans love him.

Read full article →

MEIKO

CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2004

The very first Japanese Vocaloid released by Crypton. MEIKO paved the way for the CV Series, though her early reception was modest. Over time, she became celebrated for her powerful, versatile voice — capable of both pop brightness and mature depth. Fans admire her pioneering role and the strength she brings to any performance.
