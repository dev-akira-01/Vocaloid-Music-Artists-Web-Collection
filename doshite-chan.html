Doshite-chan (どうしてちゃん) is a quirky mascot character created by VOC producer PinocchioP in 2010. It is beloved in Vocaloid fandom for its strange mix of cuteness and dark humor, reflecting PinocchioP’s style of blending playful visuals with unsettling themes.

Concept

Doshite-chan is a being born from curiosity. She appears wherever a question is asked, feeding on the endless “whys” of the world. Yet not all mysteries can be solved — when confronted with the incomprehensible, she cuts them away with her razor blade. Her worries weigh heavily on her, spilling out as bloody tears, a reminder that relentless inquiry can be both enlightening and painful.

Apperance

Doshite-chan’s appearance is deceptively simple yet deeply symbolic. Her chibi-sized body, white skin, and pink hair make her look playful, but her black lips, fangs, and question-mark tail reveal her strange nature. The razor blade she carries is not just an accessory but a tool for cutting through incomprehensible questions. Her wide eyes, lined with black eyeliner, stare with endless curiosity, while her occasional bloody tears remind us of the emotional toll of living in a world of unanswered “whys.”

Trivia

  • Doshite-chan first appeared in a Pixiv post by PinocchioP in 2010, making her one of his earliest mascots.
  • She has her own theme song, Doushite-chan no Theme, sung by Hatsune Miku.
  • Fans often represent her with the emoji ( ◉о◉ ), reflecting her wide-eyed, questioning look.
  • Her tail is shaped like a question mark, symbolizing her endless curiosity.
  • The razor blade she carries is a metaphor for cutting away questions that cannot be answered.
  • She is sometimes depicted crying bloody tears, representing the emotional toll of constant worrying.
  • Her gender is ambiguous, though she is often referred to as female.
  • Doshite-chan is occasionally paired with other PinocchioP mascots, such as Aimaina, forming a small mascot family.

Etemylogy

The name Doushite-chan comes from the Japanese word “doushite” (どうして), meaning “why.” She embodies this endless questioning, appearing wherever a “why” is asked. The suffix -chan is a casual honorific, often used for mascots or children, giving her a playful tone despite her unsettling traits.

Her design reinforces this etymology: the question-mark tail symbolizes curiosity, while the razor blade represents cutting away questions that cannot be answered.
