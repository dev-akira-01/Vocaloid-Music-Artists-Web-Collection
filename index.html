Welcome to the Vocaloid Wiki

A community-built Wikipedia for everything VOC.

VOC is a singing voice synthesizer software developed by Yamaha Corporation, first released in 2004. It allows producers to create vocal performances by typing in lyrics and a melody giving birth to an entirely new genre of music and a global community of creators and fans.

This wiki covers the Vocaloid characters, the producers and artists who use them and it's rich history. Whether you're brand new or a longtime fan, there's something here for you.

Featured Vocaloids

Vocaloid characters are more than voicebanks, they've grown into cultural icons with distinct visual designs, personalities, and massive fanbases. Here are some of the most beloved.

  1. Hatsune Miku

    CV Series • Crypton Future Media · 2007

    The face of Vocaloid worldwide.She was the first member of Crypton's Character Vocal Series and quickly became a cultural phenomenon — appearing in concerts as a holographic performer, on collaborations with global brands, and inspiring millions of fan-made songs.

    Read full article →

  2. Kagamine Rin & Len

    CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2007

    Released together as a pair, Rin and Len are described as "mirror images" of each other. Rin's voice leans bright and energetic; Len's is softer with a boyish quality.

    Read full article →

  3. Megurine Luka

    CV Series • Crypton Future Media • 2009

    Known for her deep, mature vocal tone, Luka was the first Crypton Vocaloid to support both Japanese and English phonemes. Her voice is provided by Yū Asakawa. Luka is often associated with a cool, elegant persona and is popular in genres ranging from pop to electronica.

    Read full article →

  4. KAITO

    CV Series · Crypton Future Media · 2006

    One of the earliest Vocaloids, KAITO predates the franchise's mainstream explosion. His voice is provided by Naoto Fūga and features a warm baritone quality uncommon among Vocaloids. Although initial sales were slow, he became beloved after Miku's success brought renewed attention to the entire CV Series.

    Read full article →

Featured Artists

Vocaloid's creative ecosystem is driven by independent music producers — often called P-names (e.g. "PinocchioP", "KairikiP") — who release songs on platforms like NND and YouTube. Many have gone on to professional careers or mainstream recognition.

  1. DECO*27

    One of the most prolific and celebrated Vocaloid producers. Known for emotionally charged pop songs, DECO*27 created some of the most-listened-to Vocaloid songs ever. He later transitioned into writing and producing for human artists while continuing his Vocaloid work.

    Read full article →

  2. PinocchioP

    Known for experimental, often quirky compositions with surreal or philosophical lyrics. PinocchioP has a signature lo-fi indie style that sets him apart from mainstream Vocaloid production. His work frequently blurs the line between absurdist humor and genuine emotional depth.

    Read full article →

  3. Kairiki Bear

    Recognized for dark, psychologically intense lyrics paired with energetic, distorted pop production. Kairiki Bear often explores themes of obsession, mania, and fractured identity. Their songs regularly accumulate tens of millions of plays on YouTube and NND.

    Read full article →

  4. Kanaria

    A relatively newer producer who quickly rose to fame with an immediately recognizable high-energy, dense instrumental style. Kanaria's breakthrough song became one of the fastest Vocaloid songs to reach 10 million views.

    Read full article →

  5. Iyowa

    An up-and-coming producer celebrated for combining intricate rhythmic structures with emotionally resonant melodies. Iyowa's production style draws on a wide range of influences, creating a sound that feels both fresh and deeply connected to classic Vocaloid traditions.

    Read full article →

Did You Know?

  • The name "Hatsune Miku" (初音ミクhatsune miku) which translates to "first sound of the future" — referencing both the first Crypton CV release and the idea of a new era of digital music.

  • Vocaloid songs that reach 1 million plays on NicoNico are awarded the title "Hall of Legend". 10 million earns a song entry into the "Hall of Myth".

  • Hatsune Miku has performed live concerts as a holographic projection since 2009, touring internationally and even performing alongside real human bands.

  • The term "P-name" (Pネーム) is a tradition where producers suffix their name with "P" (short for Producer), borrowed from the anime series The Idolmaster.

  • GUMI, voiced by Megumi Nakajima, was created by Internet Co., Ltd. — not Crypton — and is one of the most widely used non-Crypton Vocaloids among professional producers.
