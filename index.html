Welcome to the Vocaloid Fan Wiki

This is a community-built wiki dedicated to Vocaloid characters and the producers who create the music that shaped an entire internet era.

Whether you're here for lore, music history, character details, or producer info — this wiki brings it all together in one place.

What Is Vocaloid?

In one line: a singing voice synthesizer created by Yamaha. You type lyrics + melody → it sings.

It first released in 2004, but the world noticed it in 2007 when a teal-haired virtual idol named Hatsune Miku changed the internet forever.

Since then, Vocaloid has become a cultural phenomenon — fueled by fans, independent artists, and millions of songs posted online.

The Idea Behind This Wiki

Much of Vocaloid history is scattered across NicoNico, old forums, lost blog posts, and scattered fan sites.

This wiki aims to preserve that history — cleanly and carefully — while celebrating the creativity of the producers, illustrators, animators, and fans who built the fandom into what it is.

"Some producers started as kids posting free songs. Today they're shaping modern music. That story deserves to be remembered."

Quick Facts

  • Vocaloid launched in 2004 by Yamaha Corporation
  • Hatsune Miku debuted in 2007
  • Hall of Legend = Songs reaching 1M plays on NicoNico
  • Hall of Myth = Songs hitting 10M plays
  • "P" in producer names stands for Producer, inspired by The Idolmaster
  • Miku has performed live concerts via hologram since 2009
