About This Wiki

A fan-built Wikipedia for Vocaloid characters and the producers behind the music.

So... What Even Is Vocaloid?

Short answer: it's a singing voice synthesizer by Yamaha. You give it lyrics and a melody — it sings.

Long answer: it's one of the most creative communities on the internet, built almost entirely by independent fans and musicians.

It started in 2004. It blew up in 2007 when a virtual girl with teal twintails got her own voicebank and the internet collectively lost its mind. Her name was Hatsune Miku. You may have heard of her.

Since then? Holographic concerts. Millions of fan-made songs. Producers going from bedroom musicians to industry names. A whole genre that basically runs on passion alone.

What's on This Wiki

The Characters

Profiles on the Vocaloids themselves — who made them, and why fans are still obsessed with them decades later. From Miku to KAITO to Megurine Luka, each one has a story.

The Producers

The real backbone of Vocaloid culture. These are the anonymous bedroom musicians — posting under names like DECO*27 or Kairiki Bear — who wrote the songs you can't get out of your head. Several of them are now professional artists. This wiki covers their work, their sound, and what makes each one worth knowing.

Why This Wiki Exists

A lot of Vocaloid history lives scattered across NicoNico comment sections, old forum posts, and fan wikis.

This is an attempt to collect it — cleanly, accurately, and with the enthusiasm it actually deserves. Written by fans, for fans.

"These producers started as teenagers uploading songs for free. Some of them are now writing for artists with millions of listeners. That arc deserves to be documented."

Quick Facts

  • Vocaloid first released: 2004 by Yamaha Corporation
  • Miku's debut: 2007 — sold out in days
  • Songs hitting 1M plays on NicoNico earn "Hall of Legend"
  • Songs hitting 10M plays enter the "Hall of Myth"
  • Producer names end in "P" — short for Producer, borrowed from The Idolmaster
  • Miku has performed live holographic concerts since 2009
